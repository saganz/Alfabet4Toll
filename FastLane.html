FastLane

IT Portfolio Management (ITPM) is critical to business success, but proving its value is difficult because it is complex and time consuming.  Alfabet FastLane is easy to use and delivers value quickly at low cost and low risk. Built on 20 years of Enterprise Architecture (EA) and ITPM experience and market leadership, Alfabet FastLane turns unanswered questions into meaningful business insights—fast. This pre-defined SaaS solution requires no setup, offers guided data entry and pre-defined reports. These reports will answer the questions below and defines the reason why our customers turn to Alfabet FastLane.

GOVERNANCE

Who is responsible for our assets?

In this report you will find information about the responsible person and/or responsible organization (including their contact details) for architecture assets. Use the “not defined” tick box in order to find all objects where the responsibilities are not defined in order to fill the gap and thus drive governance within the Toll Group.

What should we be focusing on?

An application is counted in the As-Is state if the current date is between the applications Start- and End Date. An application is counted in the To-Be state if its recommendation is 'Invest' or if is a strategic application with recommendation 'Tolerate' or 'Migrate' or if the application's start date is in the future. Focus areas are highlighted with the icon: An Issue is identified if a business capability is not business relevant (Mission Critical or Business Evolving) but additional applications will be introduced in the To-Be state. Attention is required if a business capability is business relevant and additional applications will be introduced in the To-Be state.

Who owns which applications?

This dashboard shows the distribution of applications by the organisations defined as Business Owner as well as by the organizations defined as IT Owner.
Two charts are displayed; one for Business Owner Organization and one for IT Owner Organization. You can navigate directly to these diagrams by clicking on the blue headline of the reports. Click on a segment of the chart to get a preview information about the Organization, click on the Show Details button to navigate directly to object view of the Organization.

TRANSPARANCY

What is our application portfolio?

This report shows all applications with various filter options as a tabular list including all attributes and direct or indirect related objects. With this business question, Alfabet FastLane allows Toll to find out the overall application data completeness of the portfolio. Each data attribute applicable to application objects is analysed and scored to find out the overall portfolio completeness.

Which business proces area requires most IT support?

This report shows the first two business process level of Toll as nested boxes. The lowest level boxes show the Applications providing functional support to the level 2 business processes, or any of their sub processes and thus representing the AS-IS view of the application landscape in relation to business processes. Visualise any predefined application KPI‘s to get transparency of business-IT-dependencies.

What are our achitectural dependencies?

Use this report for application dependency analysis. Chose the application you want to analyze using the “Application” selector. Once you have made your selection, the selected application (colored red) and all its up- and down-stream dependencies (colored blue) are visualized. Dependencies are based on Information flows between the applications represented by the arrows connecting the applications. Information Flow properties are shown in green boxes.

Where do we have functional redundancies?

Functional redundancies in the portfolio can be reduced by analyzing applications to business capability mapping. This enables Toll to ensure that each application is supporting a business capability, there-by reducing your dependence on a small sub-set of applications satisfying organisational needs. This report shows functional redundancies between applications based on the business capability they provide. Each row shows a functional overlap percentage between source and target applications, the Business Capabilities they provide in common and which business capabilities are respectively provided by the sources and target applications only.

What is our technical debt?

Technical debt refers to the dependence of Toll's applications on technical components. This business question allows Toll to understand the application-to-component dependence based on component and application life-cycles and the defined period of usage for each object. This allows Toll to act on the technical debt before the application or component goes out of service. This Gantt chart shows all applications in an active or planned state where the lifecycle of at least one component upon which the application depends on is less than the lifecycle end date of the application itself. End-of-Life technologies not only introduce risk but drive up technical debt.

Who are our vendors?

Find out more information about the vendors and how they impact your portfolio based on provided components, supported business capabilities and the indirect impact of these vendors on applications within Toll.

RATIONALISATION

What are our cost drivers?

This business question allows Toll to analyse the organisational cost drivers. Each application operating cost is grouped up by the supported business capability. These capabilities are then represented by level 1 and level 2 capabilities in a heat-map. Capabilities are sized according to their Operational Expenditure (OpEx) for the current year. Furthermore, the color rule indicates the capabilities' business relevance. Relevance is used to mark mission critical, operating, evolving and unknown capabilities. business capabilities represented as large, bright blue boxes indicate high expenditure in non-critical capabilities whereas small, dark-blue boxes indicate low expenditure in business critical capabilities.

What are our investment/retirement candidates?

This business question provides Toll the ability to analyse their time portfolio and find out the overall portfolio recommendation for each application. In the time portfolio, Toll can find out which applications fall under the five recommendation options, namely "Tolerate", "Invest", "Migrate", "Eliminate" and "Unknown". The supported applications are based on 'Providing' application.

What is our rationalization plan?

Portfolio rationalization allows Toll to analyse their portfolio based on application expenditure. Here, costs of newly added and retired application are used to find out the organizational spending on the IT portfolio. Toll are able to find this out through the application cost over time chart and table. This dashboard shows the the number of active applications over the past and next six months. An application is considered as active per month based on its start- and endate. The light blue area shows the total number of active applications, the red one shows the retired application and the green one the new added applications.

ROADMAPPING

What is our landscape?

Toll's application landscape is an important aspect - one that help Toll in determining their portfolio roadmap. This report provides Toll with the ability to find out more about applications supporting specific business capabilities. Additionally, applications marked as strategic are uniquely identified for analysis. This report provides Toll with a snapshots of the application landscape organised by business capability at any point in time. It shows which applications are assigned to which business capabilities. It visualize any application KPI supporting rapid analysis. With that Toll are able identify risks due to potential gaps in IT support or high data transfer requirements as well identifying opportunities for rationalization activities in application dense capabilities.

What is our target architecture?

This business question provides Toll a matrix based on application to business capability and application to organizational dependencies. Target architectures are defined in Master Plan Maps which visualizes organizations on the Y-axis, business capabilities on the X-axis and the applications used by the organisations in order to accomblish the business capabilities in the middle of the matrix. Additionally, Toll has the ability to generate business supports - objects which link their business capabilities to the applications while considering the organisational structure. Business Supports allow Toll to create an organizational dependence on business capabilities, there-by gaining a unique perspective on the usage of capabilities through applications in the organisation. 

What is our roadmap?

Alfabet FastLane allows the Toll Group to get a comprehensive overview of their application lifecycle. This business question allows Toll to analyze each applications lifecycle and take corrective actions where necessary. With it you can see when applications are reaching their end-of-life and when they are planned to be implemented. Vertical arrows are showing the predecessor / successor dependencies between the applications. This report provides Toll with an overview of the application roadmap.

RISK

Where do we use sensitive data?

This business question provides Toll an overview of their applications access to sensitive data. Here sensitive data is Toll's business data that could be created, read, updated or deleted by your applications. This report shows which applications processes which business data. The applications are shown on the Y-Axis, the Business Data on the X-Axis, and the operations being performed on the business data (CRUD) in the middle of the matrix.

What is our security score?

Toll's portfolio security score is based on your evaluation of its applications. These evaluations are based on application classification indicators such as Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability. By assessing and aggregating your assessment of the applications, this business question calculates teh Toll Groups overall security score.

What is the business impact on IT Failure?

Alfabet FastLane also provides you the ability to determine your portfolio's susceptibility to IT failures. This is determined by find out the number of applications running on physical or virtual server, which applications are assigned to a business capability and finally where each device (physical or virtual) is located. By aggregating these attributes into a score, Alfabet FastLane provides Toll an overview of capability failures in case a device goes offline.
